Anxiety

Anxiety is a feeling of fear, dread, and uneasiness. It might cause you to sweat, feel restless and tense, and have a rapid heartbeat.
It can be a normal reaction to stress. For example, you might feel anxious when faced with a difficult problem at work, before taking a test, or before making an important decision.

1. Do you worry about lots of different things?







2. Do you have trouble controlling your worries?







3. Do you get irritable and/or easily annoyed when anxious?







4. Does worry or anxiety make you feel fatigued or worn out?







5. Does worry or anxiety interfere with falling and/or staying asleep?







6. Does worry or anxiety make it hard to concentrate?







7. Do you feel jumpy?







8. Do you worry about how well you do things?







9. Do you worry about things working out in the future?







10. Do you worry about things that have already happened in the past?
